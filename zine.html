02 · Welcome
Welcome

Risky behavior is framed as individual failure. Parents are expected to emotionally support their children with full financial obligations, with 46% of all new income from global economic growth since 1980 going to the richest 5%. We never catch up in our rest banks once we make it past childhood because care is an unrewarded commodity.

Deficit narratives sell. People would rather engage with what they know and like. We need designs that teach, rather than simply label.

People repeat lines as expected instead of creating genuine feedback. Even the people with the most power aren't free in their bodies. My family has achieved social mobility to the top 1% through immigration from Hispañola with education.

We are so constricted in our decisions with current social striations that our news is beginning to show wilder stories every day. Instead of teaching healthy decisions, current algorithms replicate all of our behaviors. Social stigmas are embedded in design from sourcing our histories.

Bucket sorting offers a linear model for time. We sort based on attracting what we like, and not what we learn from. Sorting allows for data intimacy.

Deterministic models of story forms that shape healing can preconfigure a pathway when data is missing using apophenia. Once the physical momentum is determined through one conformal metaphor, it is preserved through changed variables in the same category of sentiment, as determined by color.

Just like spaces prescribe how we exist within them, contexts in stories carry when data variables change. Different cultures are partial to different story maps. Color sorting with story forms allows for transversal over a spatial map of sentiment to verify message with meaning.

Mobility through structural boundaries becomes unleashed. Sorting with 13 colors applies faith to action.

Self embrace from nullifying harm by enforcing consent is the way we swim in water. Emitting and interpreting echoes, like a whale's echolocation, can help with navigational sensing.

Subjectivity is always contained in objectivity because subjects can never be removed from their objects. All color interpretations are valid, and meaningful. There is a circular gradient of meaning for complimentary opposites.

Sorting with 13 colors rejects the null hypothesis. By removing precision on labels, access becomes inclusive. Our perspectives become revealed by our choices in labeling, rather than projecting forward.

Data intimacy through play with color bucket sorting can increase access to care. I dedicate and offer this work to stewards of land and body safety. A prayer for co-liberation.

04 · Alchemize with Color
Alchemize with Color

To use this tabular, find the color meaning you want to attract or release. To attract a color, find sequences with high Gross National Happiness scores for that color — the longest bars of the matching color. To repel a color, find sequences with the lowest GNH scores — the shortest bars. The power of metaphor and story, mixed with the containment of conformal shapes, allows for controlled, bottom-up navigation.

Each RGB array within a sequence is treated as a discrete signal. When converted into complex momentum, it behaves like a wave packet:

Reading the Line Graphs
Component A
Real Part → Standing / Traveling Wave
  • Represents the in-phase oscillation
  • Smooth variation → coherent wave
  • Sharp sign changes → reflections or boundaries
  • Positive → forward propagation; negative → reverse wave
  • Symmetry → standing wave behavior
Component B
Imaginary Part → Phase-Shifted Wave
  • Represents the 90° shifted component
  • Strong oscillations → rotating or spiral wave motion
  • Zero crossings → resonance nodes
  • Large swings → phase instability or mode switching
Component C
Magnitude → Wave Energy Womb
  • Flat magnitude → energy-conserving wave
  • Spikes → localized excitation
  • Drops → damping or destructive interference
  • Nearly constant → stable wave packet (no energy loss, only phase rotation)
Component D — most important
Phase → Wave Direction & Interference
  • Linear change → traveling wave
  • Sudden jumps → phase / mode change
  • Oscillatory → standing or beating wave
  • Crossing ±π → wave inversion
Component E
Trajectory Plot → Wave Path in Space
  • Points → wave samples; lines → propagation path; color gradient → time evolution
  • Straight line → uniform propagation; zig-zag → interference or reflection
  • Closed loops → standing wave or resonance cavity
  • Knots / crossings → topological wave modes
05 · Setting the Womb
Setting the Womb:
The Knot Container

This process is not therapy, diagnosis, or performance. It is a sovereign space where shadow is allowed to exist long enough to reorganize itself, held by color, sequence, body, and consent.

00
Land & Lineage Acknowledgement
Begin by orienting to place and inheritance — a land acknowledgement honoring the ancestral and ecological context of where the work is taking place, and an intentional lineage acknowledgement informed by the Color World above. This step establishes that the work is held within a wider field of relation.
01
Identify the Block
Name, do not solve, the current block — emotional stagnation, repetitive relational patterns, somatic tension, or cognitive loops. Locate where the Knot has formed, without forcing interpretation or self-correction.
02
Reading
A short, curated reading to attune the nervous system and symbolic mind. Functions as a tuning fork for shared resonance, a permission slip to move beyond linear logic, a softening of internal surveillance. Not instructional — atmospheric.
03
Guided Visualization & Meditation
Establishes internal safety and orientation. Activates somatic awareness before analysis. Prepares the psyche to encounter shadow without fragmentation. The aim is not transcendence, but presence.
04
Knot Sequence
The core womb structure. Holds contradiction without collapse. Allows intensity to arise alongside boundaries. Prevents premature meaning-making. Supports participants in staying with what is entangled, rather than bypassing it.
05
Plot Sequence
Traces how the block came into being — examining how value, attachment, or survival strategies shaped the story; where influence or adaptation altered integrity; how movement began, stalled, or diverted. Restores narrative continuity without blame.
06
Additional Sequences (As Needed)
Pain, Sad, Fem, Direct, etc. introduced selectively depending on what colors / GNH indicators the material calls for. Not additive — responsive, offering specific pathways for different complimentary oppositions.
07
Physical Trajectory Reflection
Reflect on how the work moved through the body: shifts in posture, breath, or tension; moments of contraction or expansion; where clarity felt embodied rather than conceptual. The body is treated as data, not metaphor.
08
Discussion & Shedding
Participants invited to share selectively, with emphasis on accountability rather than confession, witnessing rather than fixing, naming what is being released and returned.
09
Closing
The container is closed intentionally — a brief grounding statement, a return to land and lineage, a reminder that integration continues beyond the session. The womb is sealed, not to trap, but to protect what has been created.
